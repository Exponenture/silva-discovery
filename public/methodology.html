Our approach
A transparent model, not a sales pitch
This tool is designed to let Discovery's team stress-test the SILVA commercial proposition using your own assumptions. Every input is adjustable. Every formula is documented here. The default values are based on industry benchmarks and publicly available South African jewellery insurance data — not optimistic projections. We encourage you to replace any default with a number you trust more. The model should be more compelling with your numbers, not ours.
How to use this tool
01
Start with the ROI Model
This assumes 100% of your book is digitised and all policyholders update their cover. It shows the theoretical maximum opportunity — the ceiling, not the forecast.
02
Then use the Adoption Model
Apply realistic opt-in rates for premium updates and VAS sign-ups. This is the more honest view. Start conservative — 10%/10% — and see where break-even sits.
03
Replace our defaults with yours
If Discovery has better data on average item values, insurance rates, or expected adoption — use it. The model is only as good as the inputs. Ours are starting points.
Portfolio assumptions — what each slider controls
Portfolio inputs
Total items in book
Default: 160,000
The total number of individual jewellery items covered across Discovery's portable possessions policies.
Default assumes approximately 2.5 items per policy across ~60,000 policies — not confirmed by Discovery. Replace with your actual item count. This is the single most impactful input in the model.
Avg premium per item / mo
Default: R100
The average monthly premium Discovery earns per individual item in the portable possessions book.
Provided by Discovery in the initial commercial discussion. Used as a sanity check — total premium book = items × R100 × 12. Not used in the ROI calculation directly.
Avg declared item value
Default: R20,000
The average value at which items are currently declared on policies — i.e., the insured value, not the replacement cost.
R20,000 reflects a specified jewellery book. Industry data suggests declared values are often 40–60% below true replacement cost. This is the most assumption-sensitive input for premium uplift — test both lower and higher values.
Underinsurance gap
Default: 50%
The percentage by which the average item is underinsured — i.e., how far below replacement cost the declared value sits.
50% is the widely cited industry benchmark for jewellery underinsurance in South Africa. A R20,000 declared item has a true replacement cost of ~R30,000, representing R10,000 of uncorrected value. This is a conservative estimate — some studies show gaps exceeding 60%.
Insurance rate
Default: 2.0%
The annual premium rate applied to insured value — what Discovery charges as a percentage of the declared value.
2% is a standard benchmark for jewellery in South Africa. Premium uplift is calculated by applying this rate to the corrected value gap. Replace with Discovery's actual blended rate for this book.
SILVA fee inputs
Platform fee / mo
Default: R45,000
The fixed monthly SaaS fee Discovery pays SILVA regardless of item count or activity. Covers infrastructure, API access, account management, and monthly portfolio reports.
This is SILVA's proposed rate. It does not scale with volume. Annual cost: R540,000.
Digitisation fee / item
Default: R30
A once-off fee per certificate when an item is first ingested into SILVA. Covers AI OCR processing, component decomposition, and template build.
Charged once only — never again for the same item. Default of R30 reflects a blended mid-tier rate. The full existing book of 160,000 items at R30 = R4.8M one-off cost in year one.
SILVA share / item / mo
Default: R3.00
The monthly revaluation fee SILVA receives per active item — regardless of whether the customer has opted into the VAS product.
Applied to the entire digitised book. At R3 across 160,000 items: R480,000/month, R5.76M/year. This is the number the VAS pass-through is designed to offset.
Gross VAS fee
Default: R7.50
The total fee Discovery charges the policyholder for the "Always On Valuations" VAS product — what appears on the customer's policy schedule.
Discovery sets this price. SILVA receives 40% (R3.00); Discovery retains 60% (R4.50). Discovery can price this higher — at R10, Discovery keeps R6 and SILVA keeps R4.
Discovery VAS share
Default: 60%
The percentage of the gross VAS fee that Discovery retains. SILVA receives the remainder (40% at default).
60/40 in Discovery's favour is consistent with standard SA insurer VAS partner economics. SILVA's 40% share at R7.50 gross = R3.00 — exactly covering the revaluation fee at defaults.
Adoption rate inputs (Adoption Model only)
Premium update opt-in
Default: 30%
The percentage of policyholders who, once SILVA revalues their item, agree to update their declared value and pay the corrected premium.
30% is a reasonable mid-point assumption. Premium uplift is only realised on opted-in items. Break-even sits at approximately 13% at R20,000 item value.
Always On VAS opt-in
Default: 25%
The percentage of policyholders who opt into the "Always On Valuations" VAS product — the named feature on their policy schedule.
VAS revenue to Discovery only comes from opted-in customers. 25% is conservative. SILVA's revaluation fee is charged on the full book regardless of VAS opt-in — the VAS is a revenue recovery mechanism, not a cost trigger.
The formulas — exactly how the numbers are calculated
Premium uplift (annual)
Uplift = Declared value × Gap % × Opted-in items × Insurance rate
The additional annual premium Discovery earns by correcting underinsured items. Applies the insurance rate to the value gap — the difference between declared value and true replacement cost.
At defaults (ROI model): R20,000 × 50% × 160,000 × 2% = R32,000,000/yr
At defaults (Adoption model, 30% opt-in): R20,000 × 50% × 48,000 × 2% = R9,600,000/yr
SILVA annual cost to Discovery
Cost = (Platform fee × 12) + (SILVA share × Total items × 12)
The total Discovery pays SILVA per year in steady state (excluding year-one digitisation). The revaluation fee is charged on the full book — not just opted-in items — because SILVA keeps all items current regardless of VAS status.
At defaults: (R45,000 × 12) + (R3 × 160,000 × 12) = R540,000 + R5,760,000 = R6,300,000/yr
VAS revenue to Discovery (annual)
VAS revenue = Gross fee × Discovery split % × VAS opted-in items × 12
Discovery's annual margin from the Always On VAS product. Only counts items where the customer has opted in. Discovery sets the gross fee; the split determines how much flows to SILVA vs. Discovery.
At defaults (25% VAS adoption): R7.50 × 60% × 40,000 × 12 = R2,160,000/yr
At defaults (100% VAS adoption): R7.50 × 60% × 160,000 × 12 = R8,640,000/yr
Net gain (year 2+)
Net = Premium uplift + VAS revenue − Annual SILVA cost
The net annual financial benefit to Discovery after paying SILVA. Year 1 also subtracts the once-off digitisation cost. Year 2 onwards is the recurring steady-state position.
At defaults, Adoption model (30%/25%): R9,600,000 + R2,160,000 − R6,300,000 = +R5,460,000/yr
Known assumptions and their direction of bias
Premium uplift is realised at renewal
The model treats premium uplift as annual and immediate. In practice, corrected values flow through at policy renewal — meaning year-one uplift may be partial depending on renewal distribution across the book.
Conservative bias
Underinsurance gap is uniform
The 50% gap is applied uniformly across all items. In reality the distribution will be skewed — some items barely underinsured, others severely so. The average may be higher or lower than 50%.
Neutral — use Discovery data if available
All opted-in items fully correct their value
When a policyholder opts into premium update, the model assumes they accept the full corrected value. Some may negotiate or partially update. This slightly overstates uplift.
Slight optimistic bias
Item count is estimated
160,000 items assumes 2.5 items per policy across 60,000 policies. Discovery has confirmed 60,000 policies — the item count should be replaced with an actual figure when available.
Replace with confirmed data
SILVA cost is charged on full book
R3/item/month applies to all digitised items, not just VAS subscribers. This is correct — SILVA keeps all items current continuously. The VAS fee is a separate revenue recovery product.
Conservative — full cost, partial revenue
No portfolio growth modelled
The model holds item count constant across years. New policyholders joining Discovery's book are not modelled as incremental revenue. In practice, growth compounds both cost and benefit.
Conservative bias
